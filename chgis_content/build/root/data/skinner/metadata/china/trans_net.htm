B. Description

1. Abstract

Coverage of arcs and nodes representing major transportation routes and destinations. Arcs include national, provincial, and local highways; railroads; and navigable rivers. Coded nodes represent central places. The coverage comprises an Arc/Info route system for network analysis using planimetric, equidistant projection, and surface distances.

2. Purpose

Spatial framework for transportation analysis at 1:1,000,000 scale.

3. Procedures Used (overview)

Source arcs were digitized by ACASIAN. At UCD, arcs in each source series (roads-pr, rails-pr, and hylp) were selected for inclusion in the transportation network, edited into a nationwide coverage, and coded. Nodes were added where arcs most closely approached each of 12,400+ points representing populated central places. Each arc was coded as to its length in an appropriate UTM projection, in which scale errors are minimized; arcs were also assigned their surface distance as calculated over a 1km-resolution digital elevation model.

4. Sources and related Data Sets

ACASIAN coverages used to assemble this coverage include the Roads-pr series, the Rails-pr series, the Hylp series, and the CPxx series.

The 1km-resolution digital elevation model used to compute surface lengths was extracted from the GTOPO-30 product (U.S. Geological Survey, 1997).

Navigable rivers were selected by UCD based on their appearance in the Atlas of China and other published sources.

Nodes representing central places are coded with the T1 identifier used in the China-T Dataset.

3. Processing steps

Source arcs (roads, railroads, and rivers) were digitized by ACASIAN. Some edgematching and other minor corrections were applied at UCD.

At UCD, arcs in each source series (roads-pr, rails-pr, and hylp) were selected for inclusion in the transportation network. Each series was edited separately to edgematch continuous arcs across provincial or tile boundaries, and then appended into nationwide coverages (roadnet, railnet, and navnet). From the Hylp series, only those arcs coded at UCD as navigable, per Atlas of China, were extracted and retained.

To accommodate transportation across major lakes and in near-coastal waters, two additional transportation components was developed. For lakes, all lakes and wide rivers from the hylp coverage which had at least one shoreline arc coded as navigable were extracted into a new coverage (navpoly). An arbitrary, regularly defined 10x10 kilometer grid was then generated and clipped by navpoly to produce a coverage named navmesh. Similarly for near-coastal waters, a twenty-kilometer buffer along China's coastline (as defined in the PONET county boundaries coverage) was created. An arbitrary, regularly defined 20x20 kilometer grid was then generated an clipped by this buffer to produce a coverage named coastwise.

The arc attribute tables for all of the components (roadnet, railnet, navnet, navmesh, and coastwise) were standardized, retaining only those items defined below. Then these components were appended to create the arc coverage transnet, which was further modified as described here.

Using NearSplit.aml, nodes were added where arcs most closely approached each of 12,000+ points representing populated central places (as shown in the CPxx series). The NAT file entry for each of these nodes was coded with the T1 identifier for its central place; other nodes are coded zero. Central places georeferenced within 350 meters of another central place may be omitted from the NAT.

Using SetEQlength.aml, arcs were coded with their planimetric length in an equidistant projection and with their length over the topographic surface of the earth. The Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) coordinate system was selected to provide the equidistant projection because it produces a scale error of not more than 0.1% within each UTM zone, and because it is more convenient to apply the well-known UTM zone system than to define other equidistant projection systems for every possible local study area in China. Arcs were first split at the boundaries of UTM zones (every sixth degree of longitude). Arcs within each UTM zone were then copied to a separate coverage and projected into the UTM projection for their zone. The length of each projected arc, as computed by the Arc/Info GIS, was then copied to the EQLENGTH item in the original AAT. In a separate operation, each arc's surface length, SLENGTH, was calculated by Arc/Info using CHDEM (a 1km-resolution digital elevation model); the surface length was then adjusted to offset the projection error by multiplying it by the ratio of EQLENGTH to LENGTH, producing SEQLENGTH.

The RCSdistance.aml script is used to generate an Arc/Info Route system and compute network costs (distances multiplied by relative cost factors estimated for various modes of transportation) between nodes based on LENGTH, EQLENGTH, and SEQLENGTH.

A. Arc Attribute Table (AAT)

CLASS

Classification of Transport Routes

See Modecodes.Dat

Rivers

01 = River, not navigable

04 = River, navigable by steamships (per Atlas of China)

05 = Lake, navigable

06 = Coastwise transport corridor

30 = River, navigable by other (JTT)

200 = River, navigable by other (TMC)

230 = River, navigable by other (JTT+TMC)

Roads

11 = Superhighways (note: these were not present in 1990 and are excluded from analysis at UCD)

21 = National highway, paved

22 = National highway, unpaved

31 = Provincial highway, paved

32 = Provincial highway, unpaved

41 = Local highway, paved

42 = Local highway, unpaved

99 = Road of unknown classification

Railroads

800-899 = Railroads (15 classifications)

LENGTH

Length of the arc

Planimetric length computed automatically by Arc/Info GIS. This is the length across the flat map surface in this coverage's Albers map projection.

EQLENGTH

Equidistant length

Planimetric length of the arc when projected into the UTM projection specified in UTMZONE. UTM projections produce a scale error of not more than 0.1% (i.e., one meter per kilometer of length).

UTMZONE

UTM Zone in which this arc falls

1..60 (in China, 43..53)

All arcs are split at UTM zone boundaries (every sixth degree of longitude).

SLENGTH

Surface length

Length of the arc when draped across the 1km-resolution DEM (in Albers projection). Computed by SURFACELENGTH CHDEM Transnet 0.004 1.0.

SEQLENGTH

Equidistant surface length

= SLENGTH * EQLENGTH / LENGTH

Attempts to offset the scale error of the Albers projection in computing SLENGTH, but may not completely overcome a projection bias within the digital elevation model.

B. Node Attribute Table (NAT)

Transnet-ID

(Alternate name: T1)

ChinaT Identifier

8-digit numeric identifer for each central place (city, town, or village) in China, as assigned in the China-T Dataset. Nodes have been added for 12,000+ central places. Other nodes (which are merely artifacts of GIS processing) are coded T1=0.
