B. Description

1. Abstract Points indicating the location of central places (cities, towns, and villages) which are included in the China-T Dataset but which are not included in the CPxx series of coverages produced by ACASIAN
2. Purpose Spatial framework for managing and analyzing tabular data compiled for central places at 1:1,000,000 scale.
3. Procedures Used (overview) Central places listed in the China-T Dataset were matched to points georeferenced in ACASIAN's CPxx coverages. This coverage was created to include those central places which were not georeferenced by ACASIAN, including urban qu centers and other places not shown on ACASIAN's source maps but which, according to the China-T Dataset, had urban populations of over 2,500. These places were then researched, using provincial atlases, the Atlas of Cities of China, local gazetteers, and other sources. Based on these sources, the places were then georeferenced relative to the position of other map features (other central places, transportation features, and county boundaries).
4. Sources and related Data Sets Other central places, transportation features, and county boundaries were originally georeferenced by ACASIAN, Griffiths University, Australia (q.v.) from the Land Use Map of China and other sources.

Urban population and China-T identification numbers derived from the China-T Dataset, University of California, Davis.

II. Data Quality Information

1. Intended area of coverage Mainland China
2. Completeness Includes points for all urban qu centers in China of 1990. Also includes points for approximately 100 other central places, which were prioritized for research and inclusion based on their urban population. Another two hundred or more small towns and villages included in the China-T Dataset are included neither here nor in the CPxx coverages.
