China GIS Data Plan

Over 800 megabytes of spatially referenced data at 1:1,000,000 scale have been assembled to support the regional analysis of China. The analysis includes the development of regional city systems, core-periphery zones, and urban-rural continuum indicies. Current work on all of mainland China focuses on two epochs: 1982-85 and 1990.  The contributions to our analysis of each set of GIS data is summarized in this diagram:

A central place analysis of 12,400 cities and towns was completed in May 1998, using demographic data in the ChinaT SAS datafile and georeferenced points in the Central Place Points GIS coverages.  This information, along with a network of transportation routes, was used to define 280 Regional City Systems within the boundaries of China's macroregions. Subsequently, socioeconomic data from the ChinaA SAS datafile is being used to analyze the core-periphery structure of these systems.  Results from this analysis are returned from the GIS to the SAS datafiles for further statistical examination.
